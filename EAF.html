Electronic waste is a growing problem. Of the $206 billion spent on consumer electronics in the U.S. in 2012, only 29 percent of the resulting e-waste generated was recycled. The rest were simply trashed. Unfortunately, this scenario happens all too often. Recently, I was told that the research lab I work in was going to be moved to a new room. This room would be smaller, so we were told to take only what was necessary. We had a lot of extra electronics in the lab - flat screen monitors, routers, USB battery packs, printers, and servers. We couldn't take all of it and we were told that what we couldn't take would be trashed. I decided to look up local charities in New Jersey that could pick up our electronics. Almost all the pieces were hardly used and most were in their original boxes.









I contacted the Electronic Access Foundation. They are a non-profit organization established for the purpose of donating surplus electronic equipment to other qualified charitable organizations in need. I let them know I had around 20 flat screen monitors, as well as other items that could use new homes. Within a week, Greg Campbell came to the lab and picked up all our recyclable electronics.







This is just the beginning. The monitors will be donated to a computer lab for a financially disadvantaged community. EAF promised to send pictures when this happens! Stay tuned!

Edit: Here they are! Our computers were donated for a Python Bootcamp. Thanks to EAF our pre-loved technology found a home for children learning to code Python!
